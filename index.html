Circus of the sun at the Futuroscope

Since its inception in 1984, the marquees of the Quebec company have stood in the great cities of the world, constantly reinventing the star trail. And now the aesthetic and poetic universe of Cirque du Soleil (CDS) invests the Futuroscope for an unexpected collaboration. "A personal shock gave birth to this adventure when I attended the 'O' show in Las Vegas. It was ten years ago. "But in the eyes of Dominique Hummel, general manager of the Futuroscope, the stars lit that evening never ceased to shine. While the park has been visited by one French person out of two since its opening in 1987, it must imagine new attractions. Enjoy the virtual sensations and the emotional register. Attracting one of the greatest producers of planetary spectacles in Vienna was a challenge. "I went to meet the teams of 45 Degrees in Montreal, two years ago, with this intuition: to us the control of the fire, the mist, the water, the lasers; To them, poetry, costume art, performance, artistic dimension. We understood that everyone could grow the other. "

The circus adapts the movie Avatar

The fairytale universe of the planet Pandora lends itself to all acrobatics. The Canadian director has teamed up with the famous Cirque du Soleque troupe of Quebec for a show of poetic and wild beauty inspired by the universe of Pandora. The planet is home to the Na'vi, blue-tinned body and long-tailed beasts with huge, feline yellow eyes, discovered in Avatar in 2009. Neilson Vignola, creative director of Toruk, explains, "The universe is the same But we have tried to do something different, which is full of eyes and gives reflection after the show. " Combining theater, acrobatics, puppets and dance in a breathtaking setting, the show tells a story that takes place several millennia before the plot of the film. Colors shimmering or issuing from an iridescence, fabulous animals and flight acrobatico-artistic make up a universe rarely equaled in the circus world. James Cameron, who prepares four sequels for his film, returns to Caesar what belongs to him: "When I turned Avatar, I used circus dancers to interpret the capture of movements and train Zoe Saldana (Neytiri). Pandora is an imaginary planet that can accommodate a thousand and one stories. "
